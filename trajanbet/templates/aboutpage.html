Free Bet Calculator

The shrine that holds the formula to matched betting. Calculates the hedge value of a price-pair for a binary fully-covered market. Further calculates the hedge size based on Bonus Bet amount provided by user. Optionally include line name and book-pair as inputs. This calculator is only applicable to bonuses with a 1x play-through requirement where stake is not returned upon winning i.e. if decimal odds for the underdog line is b, then winning a bet of size x guarantees a profit of only bx ‚àí x or x(b ‚àí 1).

Given underdog odds u, favourite odds f, and bonus bet size x, the hedge bet size y is calculated as follows:

(100 / ‚àíf) × y = (u / 100) × x ‚àí y ∴ y = ( (u / 100) × x ) / ( (100 / ‚àíf) + 1 )

Note: all odds are in American format.
To calculate profit, compute either LHS or RHS after solving for y. The hedge value is then profit divided by x (free bet size). The hedge value is the most critical metric indicative of long-term success in matched betting, as it reflects what percentage of the bonus bet can be converted into withdrawable cash risk-free regardless of the sporting outcome.

User caution: incorrect hedge values may be returned if price points for non-binary or partially covered markets are entered; for e.g. home and away moneyline for a UEFA Champions League game.

Arbitrage

In the unlikely event that there exists a price pair (American odds) u, f, such that |u| > |f|, there is a risk-free arbitrage opportunity. Arbitrage margins are generally thin ranging from 1 to 4 percent typically with ultra-rare occasions offering better margins on regular markets. Akin to promo-hedging, arbitrage needs fully-covered markets. While binary markets simplify both the math and logistics, any n-way arbitrage can be performed in theory so long as the n ways exhaust the universal event space for said market. Arbing also mandates heavy capital to guarantee small returns, for example a $2950 combined wager total may guarantee $31.5.

TBC locates arbitrage lines through low-latency parsing and flagging anytime the combined implied probability is lower than 1 i.e. should the following formula be satisfied:

∑i=1n (1 / bi) < 1.0

where n is the number of disjoint outcomes that fully cover this market, and bi is the price for each ith outcome in decimal odds.

For such an opportunity, the hedge on a $100 underdog wager is:

y = (u + 100) / (100 / (‚àíf) + 1)

Note that TBC will never locate arb pairs with both lines from the same book (regardless of whether they exist).

The presence of greater arb margins is significantly improved by using the Lay Populator trigger. This trigger repopulates the TBC database with lay-lines from betting exchanges significantly increasing the likelihood of spotting arb-lines both exchange-to-exchange and exchange-to-book. Consider an O/U on Second Half Corners in a Bundesliga game priced on average across US books at +165, -191. With narrow bid-ask spreads at exchanges, it‚Äôs possible for Smarkets to offer the Over at +177 to back. This consequently means that the Over‚Äôs lay will be ~+181. Suddenly it doesn‚Äôt seem impossible for a US operator balancing its books leading to kick-off offering a -175 for the under or perhaps a +182 on the over. Extremely liquid markets on exchanges can frequently find ~3-4% arb margins across several US sportsbooks.

DISCLAIMER: TBC doesn‚Äôt separately factor in juice for exchanges that deduct vig upon winnings. TBC also doesn't confirm sufficient liquidity on an exchange's line prior to posting lays.

Positive EV Betting

Positive EV betting in the long run will yield profits so long as sufficient volume is covered by the bettor to de-escalate short-term variance. If the true expected value of your average wager is greater than 0, the Law of Large Numbers mandates that a net profit is secured as N approaches infinity (where N is the number of bets). If the sizing for all N wagers is factored in using the Kelly Criterion with a regular ‚Äú1‚Äù multiplier, these wagers should accumulate in net winnings very close to the precise expectation.

Expected value for a wager is calculated based on a probability-weighted sum of winning and losing:

EVi = pi √ó bi ‚àí (1 ‚àí pi)

where bi is the decimal odds for bet i and pi is the vig-free average implied odds taken across at least 20 bookmakers. See more on vig-free odds below.

TBC‚Äôs odds-parser renders +EV picks ordered from highest to lowest EV, across custom-filtered books. Option to include or exclude lays in these results can be activated. Live games‚Äô EV+ lines may not update at uniform latency across all books; this is contingent on the API‚Äôs docu-parsing, which is subject to fluctuation. Akin to the Line Finder, options to filter by book and sport exist.

For game-specific EV analysis, see below for Line Shopping.

Markets historically known to provide consistent high EV picks include NCAAB moneyline, EPL O/U corners, EPL O/U second half goals, Bundesliga O/U second half goals, Serie A O/U second half goals, NFL alt. spreads and NBA player props.

Bettors must be aware that +EV betting has no short term guarantees of yield unlike matched betting and arbitrage and is only healthy when performed consistently and when used in conjunction with the Kelly Criterion. KC targets maximization of long term geometric bankroll growth:

f* = p ‚àí q / b

where p is the theoretical probability of a win, q = 1 ‚Äì p, and b is the wager‚Äôs decimal odds. f* refers to the fraction of one‚Äôs disposable bankroll that he must commit to this specific wager. KC only applies to binary outcomes, so ‚ÄúDraw No Bet‚Äù markets and some Asian lines do not apply.

No-Vig Calculator

Bookmaker overround and equivalently vigorish is the bread and butter of the multibillion-dollar sports betting industry. For a binary fully-covered market m consisting of events Œ± and Œ≤, whose book decimal odds are bŒ± and bŒ≤, the complete ‚Äúbook‚Äù Bm is:

Bm = 1 / bŒ± + 1 / bŒ≤

Equivalently, for a fully-covered market m with n disjoint outcomes:

Bm = ∑i=1n (1 / bi)

For such a book, the overround Œ© is simply Bm ‚àí 1. Recall that if Bm is lesser than 1, there is arbitrage potential since a negative overround is guaranteed profits for the bettor given the zero-sum nature of betting and life in general.

The vigourish v is then Œ© / Bm.

TBC‚Äôs No-Vig Calculator identifies the overround and vigourish for a vig-incorporated price-pair. Generally advised to use this calculator when investigating pairs from the same book. The NVC will eliminate the vig, and provide the vig-free decimal odds of events by performing:

bvfj = [ (1 / bj) / ∑i ‚â† jn (1 / bi) ]‚àí1

where bvfj is the vig-free decimal odds for outcome j in an n-way market. The exponentiation to negative one re-converts probability to decimal odds. For example, a 165, -197 vig-incorporated line has a 4.07% overround and its corresponding vig-free line is +175.7, -175.7. Notice that a vig-free market will always have its book Bm total to 1.00.

Parimutuel üêé

TBC‚Äôs gold-standard feature, the Parimutuel software, aims to facilitate man-to-man betting eliminating the bookmaker‚Äôs role as the sole layeur. It attempts to answer the question: Why bet against juiced up lines set by the powerful Monte Carlo simulations of billion-dollar books when you can bet against the masses? Or more so, against your peers.

Parimutuel betting enables bettors to wager against other bettors instead of the ‚Äúhouse‚Äù, by pooling together bets and calculating odds dynamically based on the bet-composition of the pool. Rather than having a bookmaker dictate the price of an event, sporting or otherwise, the combined public belief in such an event captured mathematically will set the price.

Parimutuel pooling has been used historically for horse racing ever since 1867, when Frenchman Joseph Oller introduced this method to Parisien race tracks. Systems called automatic totalisators were engineered to handle the rapid crunching of odds as betting volumes began to grow under the parimutuel ecosystem. TBC‚Äôs python scripts mimic a totalisator for its dynamic recalculation of odds based on new wagers.

Consider a parimutuel market with n outcomes and a bettor j‚Äôs wager on outcome a. His payout upon winning is then:

pj = xj + (1 ‚àí xj / ∑i=1ka xi) √ó P

where P is the dollar value of the total prize pool at kick-off, ka is the number of wagers on outcome a, xi is the bet size bettor i has committed to outcome k, and pj is the potential payout with stake. In bookmaking terms, the formula provides the product of decimal odds and wager size. Real markets are settled after the concerning game concludes.
